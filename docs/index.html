    ABOUT
    ME.

    Busy lifestyles mean we are constantly on the go, and this takes away fun from our everyday lives. I believe that living with beautifully designed products & services can bring us incredible joy, especially when they are a part of our daily lives.

    I used to love being surrounded by products until I realized that in recent decades services are becoming a dominant part of our economy. Information Technologies & mass digitisation increase the possibilities for services to address the basic needs of our lives. This process has been accompanied by changes in lifestyles, customs, and the culture of liquid consumer expectations. This is when i decided to shift my focus solely on products to a product-service-system design.

    I have been growing as a Service Designer since then and I love being a part of the Service family. I like being surrounded by books and post-it filled walls with research papers and sharpie pens all over my work desk. Critical Thinking and Problem Solving are my forte, it's the first step I take towards looking at the bigger holistic picture. As a Service Designer, I have a keen interest in understanding how people think, feel and behave when they interact with any product, service or system. As this allows me to deliver an end-to-end design solution that is thoughtful and connects people’s behaviours and needs to the proper functional and emotional features.
    I want to be a game changer!
    I strongly aspire to be a designer who designs experiences that are useful, innovative and most importantly delightful to the user. Experiences that wrap themselves around their lives and evolve with usage. Be it tangible in terms of products or intangible services, you simply cannot put a price on memorable and meaningful interactions. Overall, I want to deliver holistic experiences where both users and business benefit and grow by solving problems in a smart and sustainable way.
    I want to live a life that is seamless and stress free and create designs that bring a smile on people's faces. However “I don’t live to design, I Design to live” as I believe that the people do not need more stuff, but they need better and ultimately a better designed world.
